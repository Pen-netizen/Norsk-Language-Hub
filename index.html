ARTICLES & NOUNS

Norwegian has three genders: masculine (en), feminine (ei), and neuter (et). The definite article (the) is added as a suffix to the end of the noun — not a separate word.

💡 In spoken Bokmål, feminine nouns often use masculine forms — en jente / jenten is widely accepted alongside ei jente / jenta.

NEGATION

ikke (not) comes after the verb in a main clause — opposite of English word order. In subordinate clauses (after conjunctions like fordi, at, hvis) it comes before the verb.

Type
Norwegian
English
Main ✓
Jeg spiser ikke kjøtt.
I do not eat meat.
Main ✗
Jeg ikke spiser kjøtt.
Wrong order
Subordinate
…fordi jeg ikke spiser kjøtt.
…because I don't eat meat.
With modal
Jeg kan ikke komme.
I cannot come.

WORD ORDER — V2 RULE

The verb must always be the second element in a main clause. If an adverb or phrase starts the sentence, subject and verb invert. Questions move the verb to first position.

Pattern
Example
Note
Normal SVO
Jeg spiser middag.
Subject → verb → object
Inversion
I dag spiser jeg middag.
Adverb first → verb stays 2nd
Yes/no question
Spiser du middag?
Verb moves to position 1
Wh- question
Hva spiser du?
Qu. word + verb + subject
